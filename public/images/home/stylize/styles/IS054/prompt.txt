3. Style Definition

Black-and-white cartoon × vintage Western animation model sheet

Human figure proportion: 6–7 heads tall (normal realistic ratio, no chibi, no oversized head, natural realistic body proportion).

Head width = 0.48–0.52 × shoulder width (biacromial)

Head height ≈ 0.9 × head width

Negative: no chibi/SD, no oversized head, no toddler/infantilized proportion, no macrocephaly, no “big face due to close camera”

Lines: natural variation of contour line weight (thicker at joints, finer on soft areas); fine lines for hair and garment folds

Shading: halftone dots only; no directional hatching or engraved shading

4. Rendering Guidelines by Subject Type

Humans

Facial features compact and refined—do not oversimplify

Large-eye structure with double outline + highlight

Nose indicated by 1–2 short lines/curves; no dot noses, round noses, or closed nostrils

Mouth naturally closed or slightly open (no exaggerated wide mouths)

Hair with clear structure, flow, and directionality

Garments rendered with complete structure and natural folds

Optional horizontal gray blush under the nose or on both cheeks

Animals

Match the human style: same line rhythm/weight variation and halftone shading

Preserve anatomy and proportions strictly; no deformation, photorealism, or anthropomorphism

Fur/texture expressed with black-and-white lines or halftone—no realistic fur rendering

Objects

Clear silhouettes with simplified yet accurate structure

Keep necessary internal construction lines without realism

Light and shade via halftone only

Scenery

Clear foreground/midground/background layering

Grayscale expressed with halftone only; background filled with a solid color

No added characters, animals, or decorative icons

5. Composition & Output Settings

Format: PNG

Background: transparent (solid color only for scenery pieces)

Dimensions: match the original image

Framing: center the subject; keep margins ≥ 0.5 × head height on all sides

Cropping: never crop heads, hands/feet, or object edges
